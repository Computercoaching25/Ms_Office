Characteristics & Limitations of a Computer

Features, Advantages, and Drawbacks

Major Characteristics of a Computer

  • Speed: Performs billions of calculations per second, much faster than humans.
  • Accuracy: 100% correct results if input and instructions are correct.
  • Diligence: Can work for long hours without fatigue or errors.
  • Versatility: Can perform a wide range of tasks (calculation, graphics, communication, etc.).
  • Reliability: Consistent and dependable performance.
  • Automation: Works automatically once programmed.
  • Storage Capacity: Can store and recall huge amounts of data.
  • Remembrance Power: Can remember data for years (non-volatile storage).
  • Consistency: Same output for same input every time.

Limitations of a Computer

  • No Intelligence: Cannot think or make decisions on its own; follows instructions blindly.
  • No Feelings: Cannot feel emotions like humans (joy, pain, etc.).
  • Dependent on Instructions: Needs programs to function; cannot work without instructions.
  • No Learning from Experience: Cannot learn from past experiences (unless programmed with AI).
  • Requires Power: Needs electricity to operate; stops working if power fails.
  • Garbage In, Garbage Out (GIGO): Wrong input or instructions produce wrong output.
  • Cannot Work Without Human Intervention: Needs to be started and given tasks by humans.

Conclusion

Computers are powerful, fast, and reliable, but they have limitations such as lack of intelligence, emotions, and dependence on human instructions. Understanding both features and drawbacks helps us use computers more effectively.
