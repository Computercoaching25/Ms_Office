Overview

The history of computers is divided into generations, each marked by major technological changes in hardware and software.

Generation Period Main Technology Features Example
First 1940-1956 Vacuum Tubes Large, slow, high power, programming in machine language ENIAC, UNIVAC
Second 1956-1963 Transistors Smaller, faster, less power, assembly language IBM 1401, CDC 1604
Third 1964-1971 Integrated Circuits (IC) Even smaller, more reliable, high-level languages IBM 360, PDP-8
Fourth 1971-Present Microprocessors Very small, very fast, personal computers, GUI IBM PC, Apple Macintosh
Fifth Present & Beyond AI, Parallel Processing Artificial Intelligence, Natural Language, Robotics Modern AI systems
